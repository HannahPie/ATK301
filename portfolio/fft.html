Food For Thought
Spring 2020

24" x 48" poster

CLIENT
Food For Thought Exhibition

CONCEPT
This poster was designed for the exhibition Food For Thought. This exhibition would showcase posters that illustrate the designers' cultural identity and heritage through informational posters centered around food. The designers were asked to choose a food that felt nostalgic and personal to them in terms of cultural identity. Then, they researched the food, its journey to their country of origin, and how it culturally impacted the region. The resulting informational posters were displayed in Milner Library.
This poster is meant to represent how food can play a major role in identity. To play off of the exhibition's title, we chose to show food traveling through the brain through neurons. All of the food that would be showcased in the exhibition is illustrated in this poster. The lines move in an organic way, representing the fluidity of identity. When thinking about color, we wanted to choose colors that reminded the viewer of food in a general way. Red, orange, and green are the most commonly found colors in food. We wanted the text to feel tangible, as if the viewer could interact with and bite it through their eyes. This is why we chose text with rounded edges, and a sort of visual buoyancy. The text becomes one with the image as the flowing lines interact with the letterforms, flowing and weaving throughout them.
